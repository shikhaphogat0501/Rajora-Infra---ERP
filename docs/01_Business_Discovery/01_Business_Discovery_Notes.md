## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Business Discovery Document is to understand the existing business operations of Rajora Infra Homes and establish a comprehensive understanding of its organizational structure, operational processes, stakeholders, business challenges, and strategic objectives.

This document represents the Discovery Phase of the ERP implementation project and provides the business context required before requirement gathering and business analysis activities begin.

The information documented here will serve as the foundation for:

•Requirement Elicitation
•Business Requirements Document (BRD)
•Functional Requirements Document (FRD)
•User Stories

•Solution Design
•Testing Activities

## 2. Company Overview
   
Company Name - Rajora Infra Homes

Industry - Real Estate Development & Residential Construction

Business Description
Rajora Infra Homes is a regional real estate development company engaged in the planning, construction, marketing, and sale of residential housing projects.

The organization manages the complete customer lifecycle, beginning with lead generation and property booking through construction, financing, registration, and final possession.

The company also manages procurement, labour operations, inventory, customer relationships, financial transactions, and executive reporting.

Current Project - Rajora Estate

Residential Township

13. Assumptions
    
•All departments will participate in the project.
•Existing data will be migrated to the ERP.

14. Constraints
    
•Budget is fixed.
•Timeline is fixed.
•Initial rollout will cover one residential project.
•Existing data quality may require cleansing.
•Some users have limited technical experience.

15. Risks
    
•Risk	Impact	Mitigation

•Project delays	Medium	Regular project monitoring

16. Conclusion

The Business Discovery phase provided a comprehensive understanding of Rajora Infra Homes' current operations, organizational structure, business processes, and strategic goals. The analysis identified several operational inefficiencies, including fragmented data management, manual reporting, duplicate records, and limited cross-functional visibility.

The proposed Rajora ERP will address these challenges by integrating Sales, CRM, Construction, Finance, Procurement, Inventory, Labour, and Reporting into a centralized platform. This document establishes the baseline for the next phase of the project—Requirement Elicitation—where detailed business requirements will be gathered, validated, and documented before preparing the Business Requirements Document (BRD).
